My Story

From First Etch to
Full-Time Craft

I started glass etching as a hobby — a way to make something with my hands that wasn't on a screen. The first piece was a pint glass with a buddy's car on it. He put it on his bar, his friends asked where he got it, and before I knew it I had a waiting list.

That was the beginning of Petaluma Glass Works. What started as weekend projects in my garage turned into a full workshop and a growing roster of clients across Sonoma County and beyond.

"I don't do templates or mass production. Every piece is designed and etched individually — that's the whole point."

I work with car guys who want a statement piece for their garage. I work with brides who want personalized glassware for their vineyard wedding. I work with businesses that need awards that don't look like they came from a catalog. The common thread is that every client wants something that's actually theirs.

My workshop is right here in Petaluma. I meet with clients locally, and I ship carefully packed orders to folks further out. Whether it's a single etched rocks glass or a 200-piece wedding set, I give it the same level of care and attention.
